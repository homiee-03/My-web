NEET UG Counselling: A Step-by-Step Guide

  • Understanding the Counselling Process Get familiar with the key players: the Medical Counselling Committee (MCC) for All India Quota (AIQ) and the respective state authorities for the State Quota. Understand the different rounds (Round 1, Round 2, Mop-Up, etc.) and their rules.
  • Essential Document Checklist Keep your documents ready and verified. This includes your NEET admit card, rank letter, Class 10/12 mark sheets, category certificate (if applicable), ID proof, and passport-sized photographs. A missing document can cause huge problems.
  • Strategic Choice Filling & Locking This is the most critical step. Don't just fill in colleges based on last year's cutoffs. Prioritize your list based on your personal preference for college reputation, location, and infrastructure. Always have a mix of top-tier, mid-range, and "safe" colleges.
  • The AIQ vs. State Quota Dilemma Analyze your All India Rank (AIR) and your State Rank. For top-rankers, AIQ offers access to the best colleges across India. For others, the State Quota can be a golden ticket due to reservation policies and lower competition for a higher number of seats.
  • Understanding Seat Allotment Results Once results are out, you will have options: 'Accept' the seat and proceed with admission, or 'Upgrade' to a better choice in the next round. Understand the rules for 'Free Exit' vs. 'Forfeiture of Security Deposit'.
  • Post-Allotment Formalities After securing a seat, you must report to the allotted college within the stipulated time for physical document verification and completion of admission formalities. Don't miss these deadlines!
